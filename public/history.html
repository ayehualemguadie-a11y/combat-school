History of Combat Technic School

Combat Technic School was established in 1970 E.C. at Awash Arba following the need to train highly skilled military technical personnel capable of operating tanks, artillery, air-defense systems and armored vehicles.

The school location was selected because it provides excellent terrain for live-fire exercises, combined arms training, and strategic access for military leadership and logistics.

The first commander responsible for establishing the school was Major Ali Haji Abdullahi, who later became a Brigadier General. The first tank training course officially graduated on 21 Hidar 1971 E.C.

In 1972 E.C. the institution officially became known as Combat Technic Service School. Training expanded to include:

  • Tank Operations (T-55, T-62, T-72)
  • Artillery Systems
  • Air Defense Weapons
  • Anti-Tank Weapons
  • Armored Personnel Carriers
  • Military Driver Training

Beginning in 1974 E.C., Soviet military advisors helped modernize the school by introducing new training manuals, technical charts, simulators and advanced firing ranges, significantly improving the quality of education.

Following the governmental transition in 1983 E.C., the school resumed its mission by retraining military professionals and later introduced Chinese armored vehicle training programs, including the Type-89 and WMZ551B armored vehicles.

Over more than four decades, Combat Technic School has graduated thousands of military professionals from Ethiopia as well as trainees from Rwanda, South Sudan, Sudan, Somaliland, Somalia and Burundi.

Today, Combat Technic School remains one of Ethiopia's leading military technical training institutions, equipped with modern classrooms, simulators, firing ranges and experienced instructors dedicated to developing disciplined and highly skilled military personnel.
